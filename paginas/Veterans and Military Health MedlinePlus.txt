<section id="topsum_section">
    <div class="summary-title">
     <h2>Summary</h2>
    </div>
    <div id="topic-summary"><p>Military service members and veterans face some different health issues than civilians. During combat, the main health concerns are life-threatening injuries. These include</p>
<ul>
<li>Shrapnel and gunshot wounds</li>
<li>Lost limbs</li>
<li><a  name="noreveal" href='https://www.nlm.nih.gov/medlineplus/headinjuries.html'>Head</a> and <a  name="noreveal" href='https://www.nlm.nih.gov/medlineplus/traumaticbraininjury.html'>brain</a> injuries</li>
</ul>

<p>There may also be a risk of health problems from exposure to environmental hazards, such as contaminated water, chemicals, and infections. </p>

<p>Being in combat and being separated from your family can be stressful. The stress can put service members and veterans at risk for mental health problems. These include anxiety, <a  name="noreveal" href='https://www.nlm.nih.gov/medlineplus/posttraumaticstressdisorder.html'>post-traumatic stress disorder</a>, depression and substance abuse. <a  name="noreveal" href='https://www.nlm.nih.gov/medlineplus/suicide.html'>Suicide</a> can also be a concern.</p></div>
</section>
