This medication has not been studied in pregnant women, but it is possible that it may also cause birth defects in babies whose mothers received telavancin injection during pregnancy. You should not use telavancin injection while you are pregnant or plan to become pregnant unless your doctor decides that this is the best treatment for your infection. If you can become pregnant, you will need to have a pregnancy test before beginning treatment with telavancin injection. You will also need to use an effective form of birth control during your treatment. You may be excused from meeting these requirements only if you have not menstruated for 24 months in a row, your doctor says you have passed menopause (change of life), you have had a tubal ligation (''tubes tied''; surgery to prevent pregnancy), or you have had surgery to remove your uterus and/or both ovaries. If you become pregnant while using telavancin injection, call your doctor immediately.
