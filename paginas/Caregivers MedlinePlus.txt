<section id="topsum_section">
    <div class="summary-title">
     <h2>Summary</h2>
    </div>
    <div id="topic-summary"><p>Caregivers provide help to another person in need. The person receiving care may be an adult - often a parent or a spouse - or a child with special medical needs. Some caregivers are family members. Others are paid. They do many things:</p>
<ul>	
<li>Shop for food and cook</li>
<li>Clean the house</li> 
<li>Pay bills</li> 
<li>Give medicine</li> 
<li>Help the person go  to the toilet, bathe and dress</li> 
<li>Help the person eat</li> 
<li>Provide company and emotional support</li> 
</ul>

<p>Caregiving is hard, and caregivers of chronically ill people often feel <a  name="noreveal" href='https://www.nlm.nih.gov/medlineplus/stress.html'>stress</a>. They are "on call" 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.  If you're caring for someone with mental problems like <a  name="noreveal" href='https://www.nlm.nih.gov/medlineplus/alzheimerscaregivers.html'>Alzheimer's disease </a> it can be especially difficult. Support groups can help.</p>

<p class="attribution">Dept. of Health and Human Services Office on Women's Health</p></div>
